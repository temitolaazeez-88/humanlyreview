Frequently asked questions

Clear answers before you submit, pay, or track your academic review.

What can I submit?

Academic documents only: essays, dissertations, theses, journal articles, research papers, proposals, coursework, reports, portfolios and similar scholarly work.

Can I submit videos, adverts or social media content?

No. HumanlyReview is strictly for academic written documents and academic support files.

How many files can I submit at once?

One main academic file per review submission. Optional rubric, model and brief files can be attached separately when needed.

What is a file credit?

A file credit pays for one main academic document review. Credit packs can leave remaining credits on your dashboard.

What file types are accepted?

PDF, DOC and DOCX. Optional guide files are limited to 10 MB each.

Can I choose my referencing style?

Yes. Common academic styles are available, including APA, MLA, Harvard, Chicago, IEEE, Vancouver, OSCOLA and journal or institution style.

Do I need a marking rubric?

No. If you have one, upload it before continuing. If not, the review can proceed using your document and selected academic requirements.

Can you follow an example document?

Yes. Upload a model or emulation document when available. It helps the reviewer understand the expected standard and structure.

How do I receive the report?

The completed PDF reports are sent to your verified email and the dashboard status is updated.

What happens if information is missing?

The dashboard will show the status, and the team can email your verified address asking for the missing file or detail.

Can I request full editing after review?

Yes. After the report, you can request full expert editing. Pricing is based on score, feedback complexity and scope.

Is payment secure?

Payment is verified on the server before any review is marked paid or queued.
